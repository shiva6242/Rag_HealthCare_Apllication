Hospital Policies and Procedures

This document outlines the general policies followed in hospitals to ensure patient safety,
quality healthcare delivery, and ethical medical practices.

Patient Admission Policy:
Patients are admitted based on medical necessity, physician recommendation, and availability
of hospital resources. Emergency cases receive immediate priority. All patients must provide
valid identification and insurance details during admission.

Infection Control Policy:
Hospitals follow strict infection control measures including regular hand hygiene,
use of personal protective equipment (PPE), sterilization of medical instruments,
and isolation of infectious patients when required.

Medication Administration Policy:
Medications must be administered only by authorized healthcare professionals.
Dosage, timing, and route of administration must strictly follow physician orders.
Any medication errors must be reported immediately.

Patient Confidentiality:
All patient records are confidential and protected under healthcare privacy laws.
Access to patient information is restricted to authorized personnel only.

Discharge Policy:
Patients are discharged after physician approval. Discharge instructions include
medication guidance, follow-up appointments, and lifestyle recommendations.
